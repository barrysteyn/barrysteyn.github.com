The Economy

Economy is a greek word of origin and is defined as “the production, distribution (or trade), and consumption of goods and services”. There are several forms of an economy, but this explanation pertains to a market based economy

A market based economy is when goods and services are produced according to demand and supply between participants. There are three main forces which govern an economy:

  1. Productivity growth
  2. Short term debt cycle
  3. Long term debt cycle

These forces are produced by human desire to participate in transactions. Transactions therefore drive the economy.

Transactions

A transaction results from human desire/need. One party will exchange something for a good or service with another party. To the party who initially has the good or service, the something that is exchanged is cash. To the party who wants the good/service, that something is either cash or credit.
