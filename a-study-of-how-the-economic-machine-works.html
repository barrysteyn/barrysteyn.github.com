The Economy

Economy is a greek word of origin and is defined as “the production, distribution (or trade), and consumption of goods and services”1. There are several forms of an economy, a few examples are:

  • Market based economy - goods and services are produced according to demand and supply between participants.
  • Command based economy - a central agent (normally a government) commands what is produced and how it is sold and distributed.
  • Green economy - growth in income and employment are driven by investments that reduce carbon emissions and pollutions and increase biodiversity.

The video is explaining a market based economy. An absolute fundamental aspect of such an economy is demand and supply of goods and services which govern the price of transactions. Three main forces govern a market based economy:

  1. Productivity growth
  2. Short term debt cycle
  3. Long term debt cycle

These forces are produced by human desire to participate in transactions. Transactions therefore drive the economy.
